Innovation

Research & Development

New figures reveal that a record of $26.25 billion was spent on research and development alone in 2022, a rise of about four billion from 2021. These expenditure figures were published by the statistics website 'Statista'. This new development budget spent has led to the release of various new Apple products such as the new verions of iPhone, iPad and MacBook.

Apple's research and development activities are focused on innovation and developing new technologies. With the companies history of developing groundbreaking technologies that have revolutionised the tech industry, we can only imagine that this is all due to their extensive research into new hardwares and softwares as well as their focus on artificial intelligence, augmented reality, autonomous systems and health technologies.

Major Technological Innovations

Image Product Launch Date Description
Apple II 1977 The company's first mass-produced microcomputer and became one of the most successful personal computers of its time.
iPhone 2007 The company's first smartphone, that also set the new standard and its success helped to establish Apple as a leader in the mobile industry.
iPad 2010 iPads worked as a space between a laptop and a smartphone by being a not-so-little but valuable device for streaming, surfing the web, making notes, and reading e-books
iPod 2001 A revolutionary product of its time, the iPod beat out the 90s MP3 players with its incredible ability to hold 1,000 songs in your pocket.
AirPods 2016 Wireless earphones with a magnetic case.
MacBook 2006 A sleek designed laptop that ran on Apple's Mac OS X operating system, with an Intel Core Duo processor and a 13.3" widescreen display.
AirTag 2021 AirTag is a revolutionary tracking device built to act as a key finder for physical objects such as keys or bags and can be even used as a pet tracking device.
Apple Watch 2014 A smartwatch with advanced specs in an ultimate design that went on to disrupt the watch industry.
Siri 2011 Siri was the first virtual assistant with a voice and made interaction with gadgets more "human-like".
Mac Pro 2019 A central processing unit that pushes all the limits and abilities of a MacBook and even the iMac.

Future Projects

Apple has proposed their plan of transitioning from Intel processors to its own silicone. With this in mind it means that Macs are in need of a second generation Apple silicon update. The following are future projects we can expect:

  1. 15-inch MacBook Air
  2. M2 iMac
  3. M2 Mac Pro Ultra
  4. iPad Mini 7th Generation
  5. iPhone 15
  6. AR Glasses/Mixed Reality Headset
